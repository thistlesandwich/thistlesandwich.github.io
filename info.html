Useful links

Freecycle
This is a great way to get rid of stuff that still has some use left, without leaving your home! You post a photo and description of the item along with your approximate location, and if someone would like it, they email you and come and pick it up from your door.

eBay
If you would like to sell items, eBay is easy and fun. If you would like help with listing items, I can offer advice and get you started.

Argos storage
If you find you need additional storage in your home, I have found that Argos does the best value for money, simple and smart storage furniture.

Google Drive and OneDrive
If you need multi-platform access and versatile (online) data storage, Google Drive and OneDrive are great options. I can advise what would be most suitable for you, help you set up your account and teach you how to manage your data using these tools.

Youtube inspiration

Online videos are a great way to learn about organising and minimising; everyone has a different method and mindset – and so they should, as we are all different! – so it is great to be able to access a wide range of ideas so easily. Below are a few of my favourite Youtube channels that have given me inspiration and ideas over the last few years.

Messy Minimalist
Rachel has an enormous amount of possessions filling up her house, and in 2016 she made the decision to go through every item and decide whether to keep it or say goodbye. Her videos often document her going through items and chatting with us about some of the more amusing items she finds.

Jenny Mustard
Jenny is a minimalist, she loves white, clean and empty spaces. Even though her lifestyle isn't for everyone, she has some great and very sensible ideas, which can be easily incorporated into our own lives.

Happily Ever Minimal
Alycia is a practical minimalist with a focus on a waste-free lifestyle. She has some particularly great videos about making your own products out of entirely natural ingredients, whether they are cleaning products or cosmetics.
